Types Of farming

Types Of farming

Arable farming

In these types of farming ,crops are grown on a fixed rainfall of around 50 to 100 cm. In some parts of India , kharif and rabi crops are grown in two seasons. Organic fertilizers are widely used for such crops. Also

agro-farming is done on a large scale using mulch.Agriculture is practiced in many states of India. Although India has 70 per cent water, most of the agriculture in India falls under this type of arable agriculture.



Types Of farming

Poor farming

In this type of farming poor farming is done in areas with less than 50 cm of fixed rainfall. Moisture is maintained in the soil, and this poor farming is mostly based on the problems of land conservation. Dehydrated farming is water scarcity farming. Mainly there is seasonal farming in rainy season. The choice of crops grown in this field is also limited.

Crops are grown in this field using some of the specific recommendations of the cultivator to protect the soil and retain moisture.For example:Parallel planting of crops by leveling dams, low seed sowing, limiting the number of seedlings in the field, maturing by strip sowing method, use of mulch and moderate use of fertilizers. Etc. Recommended farming is done using the recommendations.


Types of farming

Horticulture farming

Horticulture is not a type of farming but a method of harvesting crops. Crops in this field are not mainly dependent on rainfall. Hence the production of these crops are more than arable crops.Due to enough water supply, these crops are grown allround.

Glacier farming is done by storing a large amount of water. The problems of agriculture are completely different from agriculture. In this type of farming , type of fruit is the main product. Depending on the climate and rainfall in Konkan, mango, coconut, cashew, betel nut etc. This types of fruit are main products . Lemons in plateau areas of Maharashtra, Oranges,all citrus fruits are cultivated. Grapes are cultivated if water is available in dry climate.

Types Of farming

Vegetable farming

Vegetable farming is a type of horticulture. With definite rainfall, availability of irrigation facilities and market suitability, this type of land and other resources can be fully utilized. Since vegetables grown in this type of farm are perishable in nature, it may be beneficial to grow crops if it has fast transport facilities.

Types Of farming

Livestock and Dairy based agriculture

Livestock and dairy farming is practiced in any region which is not conducive to crop production in spite of favorable conditions i.e if the region is profitable for animal husbandry and animal protection and dairy

business. For this , grazing animals and land with irrigable facilities like fodder, cereals, food etc. are required. In addition, there should be facilities for easy and cost effective sale of livestock and dairy products. This condition is conducive to this type of farming.


Mixed farming methods:

Mixed farming means farming with crops and livestock. In this mode, crops are grown for cash and animal husbandry is also done.- This type of farming has become very widespread and is recently being done. For fish farming, they dig up the soil, make large ponds and release water in them. They release fish seeds in this pond. For this, freshwater fish species are recommended. The fish are reared in a scientific manner for optimal growth.

Types Of farming

Organic farming

Crops in this type of farming needs nutrients from the soil. This farming is done by replenishing the used nutrients in the soil. Foodstuffs are also used extensively to achieve the goal of increasing productivity.

Palapachola ,Green crops like hemp or dhancha, which are decomposed in the soil, are buried in the soil, and manure and compost are used for fertilizer crops. Also all other types of plant organic matter replenish the nutrients used by mixing and decomposing in the soil. Thus crops are grown in nutrient rich soil.


Types Of farming

Chemicals farming

Made using only chemicals.Outgoing agriculture has its limitations for productivity growth. Therefore, to overcome this, farming is done using chemical fertilizers and production is increased. Also chemical pesticides for disease and pest control and pesticides are used. This type of farming increases the yield for some time but reduces the quality of the grain produced. In addition, the chemicals in the human body from grains are harmful to health.


Types Of farming

Green House farming

Low greenhouses are used to extract maximum yields from the area, and to produce cash crops with maximum economic benefits by having complete control over natural factors like soil, climate, heat, humidity, moisture, etc.


Types Of farming

Nursary farming

Nursery farming is required as a pre-preparation for horticulture and vegetable farming. Given this need, some progressive farmers only cultivate nurseries.
